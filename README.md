***
**<span style="color:green">Imelda</span>**

Tech, music, film, iron, and [handrolls](https://www.opentable.com/r/handies-douzo-montrose-houston) — roughly in that order.
***

I've been living inside technology since 1995, when I landed my first job at the Wall Street Journal and realized I'd accidentally found my calling. I'd studied Criminal Justice and Psychology — had my eyes on law school — but the moment I touched enterprise tech, something clicked that courtrooms never did. Thirty-something years later I'm still here, still learning, and I wouldn't trade a single upgrade cycle.

These days I work in Microsoft 365 — SharePoint, Teams, Exchange, the whole ecosystem — and I'm currently deep in certification prep because in this field, standing still isn't really an option. I'm also a 9-year competitive Masters [Olympic weightlifter](https://www.wolfestrengthhouston.com/) with international experience, which probably says as much about how I approach work as it does about what I do on weekends.

I grew up with a camera in my hands. My dad brought back a Minolta from Japan and handed it to me when I was a kid, and that was it — I've been visually wired ever since. No particular style, no rules. I've shot what moved me, sold prints, shown work, and kept going. That same Minolta lives on a shelf now. He also gave me his Polaroid when he retired, and we recently tracked down film for it so my sons and I could experiment together. Film is expensive, it turns out. Worth it.

Music is another language for me. Doolittle by the Pixies came out when I was in 9th grade and we wore out the tape in study hall. I used to say if I ever won the lottery I'd pay for a private Pixies concert — and then they got back together and I saw them four times instead, which is better. I'm deep into the BritPop world: the Smiths, New Order, Oasis, Radiohead. Dermot Kennedy four times and counting. Morrissey twice with my son. And then there was the Oasis trip — 12 hours notice, Newark, 38 hours total, one of the best decisions I've ever made. We still walk through the house saying "remember when we saw Oasis?" We do. We always will.

I'm a 30-year pescatarian, a longtime [Instagrammer](https://www.instagram.com/imelda/) who's finally learned to put the phone down, and someone who believes sushi — specifically handrolls — is the answer to most of life's harder days. I'm a Texan, a devoted Irish music session-goer, and someone with a serious pull toward Ireland that I intend to act on. I've traveled through the US, Mexico, Belize, Japan, and Ireland, and every trip has only made the list longer.

At the center of everything is my 3+1 — three kids and one FAVE, my granddaughter, who named me Ma and is hands-down my favorite person on the planet. Also [Henry](https://www.instagram.com/henrythetxgsp), our German Shorthaired Pointer and the third GSP this family has loved. It's the breed for us. Always will be.

> Microsoft 365 · 30 yrs in tech · Olympic weightlifting · film photography · Irish trad sessions · Pixies forever · pescatarian · handroll devotee · GSP mom · Texas · Ireland-bound
